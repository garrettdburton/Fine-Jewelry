Ancient Green Jasper and Antique Carnelian Pendants from the Sahara

US $: 800.00

Description

Ancient green jasper beads paired with antique carnelian pendant beads. Twenty-five of the carnelian beads are pendants and one of the green jasper beads is a triangular shaped pendant. One corner of the green jasper pendant is slightly damaged, the triangle is drilled so it does not lay flat on the body, but projects outward. The corner shows significant signs of wear, indicating that this was an old break. Each of the pendants is faced with a small jasper disc bead, 3 mm in length and 6 mm in width. These green jasper beads are extremely rare and an entire necklace of them is nearly impossible to find. The origin of these beads is obscure, and it would make a fine project to research their manufacture. They are said to come from Mauritania. My opinion is that the carnelian pendants and beads are hundreds of years old; the green jasper beads may be a thousand or more years old.

About

David Ebbinghouse has traveled widely, especially in India and has collected ancient beads and conducted research on them. He has published bead research and given lectures to various bead societies around the country. All this he considers to be a subset of his artistic practice, rather than a separate activity, as he seeks to integrate all of his experiences into his work. This intention to create an art that is instrumental in living a life in modern times is the same impulse behind all of the world’s mythology throughout all time and history. In this way Ebbinghouse seeks to create an art that transcends the boundaries of how we normally think about art.
